# Source (en-US):
# Get something from somewhere. Bring something for someone.
# 
# Eushop is the open peer layer for trips, locals, and open asks: spare luggage capacity on routes people are already taking, finder-fee shares between locals, and open asks that notify matching supply.
# 
# Three primitives, one network:
# 
# • Trip offers. A verified user announces a trip ("Munich → Warsaw, May 14, 6 bag slots"), each slot reservable at an agreed fee per slot.
# • Open asks. A buyer posts what they want; matching trips and listings in the same corridor or radius light it up automatically.
# • Local listings. Neighbourhood-scale finder-fee posts ("half a tin of Krówki, €3, pickup at Goetheplatz") with privacy-preserving 5 km cell discovery.
# 
# Why Eushop
# 
# • Privacy by design. Pins are deterministically jittered inside a 5 km cell. Your home address is never shared.
# • Profiles you can trust. Verified-bringer badge, public exchange history, in-app chat, public-handoff guidance.
# • Hosted in the EU. Hetzner Falkenstein and Cloudflare EU. GDPR is a baseline, not a feature.
# • Honest pricing. Small platform fee on each confirmed reservation: min(€1.50, 12% of finder fee). Settlement of the goods stays between you and the other party.
# 
# What Eushop is not
# 
# • Not customs, freight, or regulated payments.
# • Not a courier service, not an employer, not a delivery platform.
# • Not surveillance — we do not track you across other apps and never request the iOS IDFA.
# 
# Who it is for
# 
# EU diaspora corridors first — Polish, Romanian, Italian, Greek, Turkish, German communities moving between home and where they live now. The primitives travel anywhere people travel.
# 
# Available languages: English, Deutsch, Français, Polski, Español, Italiano (more coming as community translators ship them).
