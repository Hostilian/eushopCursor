# 🇪🇺 Food Safety & Import Compliance Report: Peer-to-Peer Sharing Model
**Audit Date: July 1, 2026**

This report analyzes the legal and regulatory compliance requirements of **EuShop** under European Union food safety laws, specifically focusing on the peer-to-peer (P2P) non-commercial exchange of specialty foods across borders.

---

## 1. Regulatory Context: Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 (General Food Law)

Regulation (EC) No 178/2002 lays down the general principles and requirements of food law in the EU. 

### 1.1 Scope & "Food Business Operator" (FBO) Definition
Article 3(3) of the Regulation defines a "Food Business Operator" as:
> *"The natural or legal persons responsible for ensuring that the requirements of food law are met within the food business under their control."*

A "Food Business" is defined as any undertaking, whether for profit or not and whether public or private, carrying out any of the activities related to any stage of production, processing, and distribution of food.

### 1.2 Applicability to P2P Marketplace
Because EuShop **does not handle, prepare, purchase, store, transport, or sell any food directly**, EuShop is categorized as an **Information Society Service** (intermediary directory) and **not** a Food Business Operator. 

For users, the European Commission’s guidance on the implementation of certain provisions of Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 states that:
* Occasional handling, preparation, storage, and serving of food by private individuals for social, community, or charitable events (e.g. school fetes, neighborhood exchanges) do **not** fall under the scope of commercial food hygiene rules.
* **Non-commercial sharing**: If travelers import chocolate, cookies, or canned specialties for family or peer exchange on a minor scale without systematic professional distribution, they are exempt from FBO status.

---

## 2. Regulation (EC) No 852/2004 on Food Hygiene

This regulation details the hygiene requirements for food production and storage.

### 2.1 The Cold-Chain Exemption
Articles 4 and 5 mandate strict hazard analysis (HACCP) and temperature controls for commercial operators. 
* **EuShop Mitigation**: To maintain compliance and prevent risk, the Platform's Terms of Service explicitly prohibit listing highly perishable fresh meats, raw fish, and unpasteurized dairy. 
* **Focus on Niche Shelf-Stable Goods**: Listed items are primarily restricted to ambient, shelf-stable commercial packaging (candies, biscuits, chocolate, canned specialties, spreads) where standard room-temperature transport does not pose a microbial safety threat.

---

## 3. Cross-Border Customs & Personal Import Allowances

Under EU customs rules, travelers entering the EU or moving between EU member states are subject to regulations regarding animal-origin products:

### 3.1 Intra-EU Movement
Within the European Single Market, there are no customs controls or limits on the transport of foodstuffs for personal use between member states, provided they comply with veterinary restrictions (e.g., foot-and-mouth disease emergency restrictions).

### 3.2 Non-EU Imports (Third Countries)
If a user lists an item brought from a non-EU country (e.g. UK, Switzerland, Turkey):
* **Meat & Dairy Ban**: EU law strictly prohibits personal imports of meat, meat products, milk, and dairy products from non-EU countries due to the risk of animal pathogens.
* **Exceptions**: Confectionery, chocolate, biscuits, and bread products are allowed without restriction, provided they do not contain meat or fresh dairy components.
* **EuShop UI Safeguard**: Filter alerts on listing creation advise users importing from third countries to tick tags declaring compliance with personal import allowances.

---

## 4. Due Diligence & Platform Compliance Recommendations

To satisfy due diligence requirements for institutional acquisition:
1. **User flagging system**: Implement a community reporting button on listings enabling users to flag suspicious, contaminated, or prohibited food items.
2. **Explicit allergen disclaimer**: Automatically render an allergen warning badge on all P2P listings: *"P2P item: verification of ingredients and allergens is buyer's responsibility."*
3. **Escrow terms**: If in-app payments are integrated, release payment only after the buyer confirms that the item's seals are intact and the food matches the described state.
